Data were reported for physical development, neurological and psychological effects, neurobehavioural and psychosocial function, kidney function, immune function, urinary mercury, urinary porphyrin excretion, and postoperative sensitivity. Most outcomes were reported in only 1 study. Overall, there was a mixed picture that was not easy to interpret, but did not seem to suggest that either composite resin or amalgam restorations are more likely to lead to clinically important harms.
