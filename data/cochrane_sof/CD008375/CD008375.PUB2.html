Movement of upper first molars (mm)

The mean movement of upper first molars (mm) in the headgear group was ‐1.04 mm (distal movement)

The mean movement of upper first molars (mm) in a distal direction for the intraoral appliance group was 1.45 mm more (‐2.74 to ‐0.15)

⊕⊝⊝⊝
very low2,3

Movement of the upper first molars in a distal direction is the desired type of tooth movement. This result indicates that the intraoral appliance is superior to headgear for this outcome

Movement of upper incisor teeth (mm)

The mean movement of upper incisor teeth (mm) in the headgear group was ‐1.09 mm (distal movement)

The mean movement of upper incisor teeth (mm) in a mesial direction for the intraoral appliance group was 1.82 mm more (1.39 to 2.24)

Change in overjet (mm)

The mean loss of anchorage (mm) in the headgear group was ‐0.86 mm (reduction in overjet)

The mean change (increase) in overjet (mm) in the intraoral appliance group was 1.64 mm more (1.26 to 2.02)
